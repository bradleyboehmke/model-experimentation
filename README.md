# ModelOps and Model Experimentation: From Training Runs to Managed Models

A practical introduction to ModelOps principles and experiment tracking for analytics and data science students.

## Purpose

This workshop teaches students how to evolve chaotic model training workflows into structured, reproducible experimentation systems. By introducing ModelOps principles and experiment tracking with MLflow, students learn to build **reliable, comparable, and reproducible** model development workflows that demonstrate professional-level engineering skills — a key differentiator when transitioning from academic projects to industry roles.

## Workshop Goals

By the end of this workshop, students will:

- Understand ModelOps as lifecycle management, not just model training
- Learn the importance of structured experimentation
- Understand what experiment tracking captures (parameters, metrics, artifacts)
- See a real-world example using MLflow with the Rossmann forecasting project
- Know how to apply these concepts to their own ML projects
- Have a roadmap for building production-grade ML portfolio projects

## Philosophy

The workshop emphasizes **structured experimentation** — moving from chaotic notebooks with hard-to-compare results to systematic tracking where every experiment is logged, comparable, and reproducible. We validate that notebooks are perfectly acceptable for learning and exploration, while encouraging students to adopt experiment tracking early to build better models faster and with more confidence.

The key message: Good modeling starts with good experimentation practices. Track your runs, compare systematically, and make reproducible decisions.

## Example Project

The workshop uses the [Rossmann Store Sales forecasting project](https://github.com/bradleyboehmke/rossmann-forecasting) as a real-world case study, demonstrating:

1. **Baseline Experiment** — Initial model with MLflow tracking
2. **Iterative Tuning** — Multiple runs with parameter variations
3. **Comparison & Selection** — Finding the best model through systematic comparison

Students see how MLflow captures experiments and enables systematic model improvement.

## Target Audience

Analytics and data science students who want to move beyond chaotic model training and build professional ML workflows that demonstrate engineering rigor and reproducibility to potential employers.
